5 top tips to help make chatter matter

Encourage your child’s oral language from the very beginning. Make the following activities part of the warm, loving relationship you are already fostering with your child:

  1. Top Tip No 1
    Talk with your child whenever possible and show a genuine interest in what they have to say (be a great listener). Use appropriate vocabulary and grammar and be a good model of speech and language.
  2. Top Tip No 2
    Respond to your child when they speak to you and wherever possible, expand on the conversation. Ask questions to find out more. Praise your child.
  3. Top Tip No 3
    Talk about the things you are doing with your child, even everyday things like cooking, shopping, emailing, gardening, or what you see when you are out driving/walking together. Make the most of every opportunity.
  4. Top Tip No 4
    Read to your child anytime, anywhere, every day. Discuss what happens in the book, talk about the characters, what happens and where it happens, ask your child to retell the story to you or another family member and make links to their own life.
  5. Top Tip No 5
    Sing songs and teach and share nursery rhymes.
